Hey team — heads up on a structural update to the customer journeys framework.

**What changed:** The journeys now follow two personas instead of one.

**Maya Torres (CX Operations Manager)** — still the protagonist for Journeys 1-2. She onboards the agent, handles daily ops/escalations, and builds new skills. This is Gorgias as it is today: a support tool for CX teams.

**Daniel Reyes (GM of E-Commerce)** — new character, enters in Journey 3 and becomes the primary user in Journey 4. He oversees the full customer lifecycle (acquisition → conversion → support → retention) and starts paying attention when the agent crosses from support into revenue.

**Why this matters for the story:**
The thesis is that as the agent unifies support and marketing through skills, the ICP shifts from CX manager → GM of ecommerce. Journey 3 (Mother's Day) is the transition point — Daniel sees one agent managing the full shopper lifecycle and realizes this isn't just a support tool. Journey 4 is now framed as "E2E Performance" through his lens — he cares about LTV, churn, and revenue retention, not individual tickets.

**Also new in Journey 2:** After the pet safety failure, Maya now reviews the proposed skill, runs an offline evaluation on 124 historical tickets, fixes edge cases, and publishes — instead of just "activating" it. This makes the eval loop consistent with Journey 4 and shows it's not just a big-scale thing.
